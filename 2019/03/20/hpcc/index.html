Abstract:

CC is the key to achieving goals of RDMA.

  • ultra-low latency
  • high bandwidth
  • network stability

From years of experiences on operating large scale RDMA networks, we find the
existing RDMA CC has inherent limitations for reaching these goals.

In this paper , we present HPCC(High Precision Congestion Control). A new RDMA CC mechanism which acheive three goals:

  • HPCC leverages in-network telemetry (INT) to obtain precise link load information and controls traffic precisely.
  • HPCC can quickly converge for utilizing free bandwidth or avoiding congestion and maintain near-zero in-network queues for ultra-low latency.
  • HPCC is also fair and easy to deploy with hardware. We implement HPCC with commodity programmable NICs and switches.

RDMA (remote direct memory access) is one promising and relatively mature transport solution.because it offloads network processing into the NIC hardware and avoids performance bottleneck of TCP/IP stacks and CPU overhead.
